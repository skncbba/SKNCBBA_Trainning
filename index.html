Banking Fundamentals (Day 1 Focus)

The Three Pillars of Banking

Private Banking (Our Focus) Retail Banking Investment Banking

Client Focus: High-Net-Worth (HNW)

Our clients are individuals and families with significant investable assets ($1M+). The focus is relationship-driven, offering tailored financial solutions, not standardized products. The Swiss advantage historically relies on stability and neutrality, appealing to international wealth preservation.

Core Services:

  • Wealth Management & Portfolio Advice (Discretionary/Advisory)
  • Specialized Credit (e.g., Lombard Loans, Structured Financing)
  • Estate, Tax, and Succession Planning (Coordination with RMs)

Call Center Relevance: HIGH

Every call requires a **concierge-level approach**. You represent the high standard of service and discretion expected by these clients.

Client Focus: General Public

This is standard consumer banking for the mass market. Driven by high volume and lower margins. The primary income source is the interest spread (charging more on loans than paying on deposits).

Core Services:

  • Savings & Checking Accounts, Mass Market Credit Cards
  • Home Mortgages & Auto Loans

Call Center Relevance: LOW

We do not manage these products in the Private Banking division. **Redirect or politely inform the client of the division focus.**

Client Focus: Corporations & Governments

Helps large institutions raise capital in the primary markets (e.g., IPOs) and provides strategic advisory services (e.g., M&A). Income is primarily fee-based, generated from large transactions.

Core Services:

  • Mergers & Acquisitions (M&A) Advisory
  • Underwriting (Issuing Stocks/Bonds)

Call Center Relevance: LOW

Handled by a separate internal division. Calls related to this are complex and **must be escalated** to the appropriate desk.

Core Investment Terms & Concepts

AUM

Total **Assets Under Management**. The key measure of a private bank's size and client trust. Fees are often charged as a percentage of AUM.

Liquidity

How quickly an asset can be converted to cash. **High Liquidity** (Cash) vs. **Low Liquidity** (Private Equity, Real Estate).

Risk & Return

The **higher the potential return**, the **higher the risk** (potential for loss). This informs client profile segmentation.

Diversification

Spreading investments across different assets (stocks, bonds, alternatives) to mitigate the impact of poor performance in any single area.

Wealth Management vs. Private Banking

These terms overlap, but for clarity, understand the distinction:

Wealth Management

A **holistic service** focused on the entire financial lifecycle, including investment strategy, tax planning, estate planning, and trust services. It's the strategic roadmap.

Focus: Strategy, Legacy, Long-term Growth. Involves a team of advisors.

Private Banking

Provides the **exclusive, tailored banking platform** needed to execute the wealth strategy (dedicated RM, special credit products, high-touch support). It's the premium service delivery mechanism.

Focus: Execution, Concierge Service, Specialized Products. Often requires a minimum asset level.

Call Center Scenarios & Escalation Mastery (Day 3 Focus)

Practice core competencies for handling HNW clients. Click each scenario to reveal the compliant, white-glove procedure. **Scenarios 2, 4, 6, and 7 are high-risk escalation procedures.**

Scenario 1: Irate Client - "My RM hasn't called back in 48 hours!"

Procedure (Tone: Empathetic, Efficient):

  1. **Verify Client & Apologize:** Authenticate the client immediately. State, "I sincerely apologize for the delay. I will personally ensure this is resolved."
  2. **Triage:** Briefly confirm the nature of the request (e.g., "Was this regarding the bond purchase?"). *Do not solve the issue.*
  3. **Soft-Escalate:** "I am immediately sending an urgent notification to the RM's direct assistant and their team leader. I will hold the line until I confirm the message has been delivered to the appropriate party."
  4. **Document:** Log the call as a priority complaint.
Scenario 2: The Compliance Red Flag - "I need to move a large sum offshore to avoid a new tax."

HIGH RISK - COMPLIANCE REQUIRED

Procedure (Tone: Neutral, Firmly Compliant):

  1. **Verify Client & Stop Inquiry:** Authenticate the client. **Do not ask for more details on "avoiding tax" or offer any legal/tax opinion.**
  2. **Refer Only:** "I understand you have complex structuring needs. Due to strict regulatory requirements (e.g., FATCA/CRS), I must connect you with your Relationship Manager, who works directly with our internal compliance and legal partners on these specific matters."
  3. **Mandatory Documentation:** Log the call details and the client's explicit request in detail, tagging it for immediate review by the Compliance Officer. **Do not give any advice or opinion.**
Scenario 3: Portfolio Inquiry - "My portfolio is down 5% this month, what should I do?"

Procedure (Tone: Empathetic, Information-Only):

  1. **Verify Client & Validate Data:** "Thank you for reaching out. I can confirm your portfolio’s market valuation is currently [State Value]."
  2. **Apply Golden Rule:** "As per compliance, I cannot offer any market commentary or investment advice on whether you should hold or adjust your position."
  3. **Escalate & Empower:** "I see a portfolio review note scheduled for next week, but I can contact your RM right now to schedule an immediate call back to discuss your strategy and risk tolerance."
Scenario 4: Specialized Product - "I need to discuss the collateral ratio on my Lombard Loan."

HIGH COMPLEXITY - CREDIT RISK INVOLVED

Procedure (Tone: Knowledgeable, Direct Transfer):

  1. **Verify & Acknowledge:** This is a high-level, complex credit product (a loan collateralized by assets). Acknowledge the term (Lombard Loan) to show proficiency.
  2. **Direct Transfer:** "That is a specialized credit inquiry. I am immediately connecting you with our Credit Solutions team/your RM to discuss your collateral ratio and any potential margin calls."
  3. **Do Not:** Attempt to look up the current ratio or discuss margin requirements unless explicitly authorized by SOP. The client must speak to a licensed lending specialist.
Scenario 5: Security Alert - Client reports an unauthorized transaction (potential fraud).

CRITICAL - IMMEDIATE SECURITY ESCALATION

Procedure (Tone: Calm, Urgent, Reassuring):

  1. **Verify Client & Freeze:** Authenticate and immediately initiate a temporary freeze on online access/outbound transfers, if possible via internal systems.
  2. **Gather Facts:** Ask: "When did you first notice the transaction, and what is the exact amount?" (Do not discuss login details or passwords).
  3. **Escalate:** "I am immediately escalating this call to our Fraud and Security Desk. Please hold the line; I will introduce you directly to the officer in charge of investigations."
  4. **Mandatory Documentation:** Do not hang up until the transfer is successful. Log the precise time and details of the reported fraud.
Scenario 6: Client asks for advice on structuring their Will or Trust documents.

LEGAL/TAX BOUNDARY

Procedure (Tone: Respectful, Expert Referral):

  1. **Verify Client:** Authenticate.
  2. **Acknowledge Complexity:** "I understand this involves sensitive legal structuring."
  3. **Soft-Transfer/Referral:** "Our role is wealth management execution; we are not licensed legal counsel. Your Relationship Manager coordinates closely with specialized estate planning attorneys. I will arrange an introductory call with your RM and our dedicated Wealth Structuring team immediately."
  4. **Avoid:** Do not comment on jurisdiction, beneficiaries, or legal terminology.
Scenario 7: Client asks about the bank's commitment to ESG/Sustainable Investing.

Procedure (Tone: Knowledgeable, Product Information):

  1. **Verify & Respond:** Authenticate. "Yes, we are highly committed to sustainable investing and ESG factors."
  2. **Provide USP Context:** (Refer to Mirabaud/Pictet facts) "We integrate ESG criteria across many of our mandates and have dedicated thematic funds focusing on sustainability (e.g., climate change, social equity)."
  3. **Escalate for Detail:** "For details on the specific ESG mandate for your portfolio, I need to connect you with the **Asset Management** division or your RM."
Scenario 8: Client is upset about the **AUM-based management fees** on their statement.

Procedure (Tone: Empathetic, Policy Explanation):

  1. **Verify & Empathize:** Authenticate. "I understand fee structures can be complex, and I appreciate you calling about your statement."
  2. **Explain Model (Simplified):** "Our fees are calculated as a small percentage of your **Assets Under Management (AUM)**, covering the continuous advisory and management services provided."
  3. **Escalate to Resolve:** "Any discussion regarding fee calculation adjustments or detailed explanation of the fee model requires your Relationship Manager. I will schedule a discussion immediately."
Scenario 9: Client asks for the latest **Julius Baer** or **UBS** market outlook report.

Procedure (Tone: Helpful, Value-Add):

  1. **Verify & Confirm:** Authenticate. "Yes, I see the latest Chief Investment Office report was just published."
  2. **Fulfillment:** "I can email you a secure link to download the report immediately, or I can ask your RM to highlight the key takeaways for you."
  3. **Value-Add:** "Were you interested in a specific sector, like technology or fixed income?" (Use their answer to inform the RM).
Scenario 10: Service Recovery - Client is worried because their long-time RM has left the bank.

Procedure (Tone: Reassuring, Stability Focused):

  1. **Verify & Reassure:** Authenticate. "I understand this is a change, but rest assured, your assets remain secure and your portfolio strategy remains unchanged."
  2. **Explain Transition:** "You have been seamlessly transitioned to **[New RM Name]**, who is [New RM's Title] and has been with the firm for [Time]. They have access to your full history."
  3. **Immediate Action:** "I will arrange an immediate, introductory call between you and [New RM Name] to ensure a smooth transition and address any urgent needs." (Avoid discussing why the old RM left).
Scenario 11: The "Soft-Transfer" Technique

Goal: Transfer a client without making them feel passed around.

Script:

"Mr. [Client], thank you for clarifying. The person best equipped to handle that is [Name/Title], who is our specialist in [Area].

May I please have your permission to place you on a brief hold while I connect you? I will personally introduce your request to them."

Scenario 12: Client asks about the minimum requirement to open a private banking account.

Procedure (Tone: Informative, Qualifying):

  1. **Verify Interest:** "Thank you for your interest. We specialize in High-Net-Worth clients."
  2. **State General Threshold (Softly):** "While the exact minimum can vary by region and the scope of services required, the typical entry point for personalized Private Banking services generally begins around **$1 Million in investable assets**."
  3. **Lead Generation:** "I can immediately connect you to our Business Development team for a discrete, complimentary discussion of your needs." (Never turn away a potential client directly).
Scenario 13: Operational - Client hasn't received confirmation for a recent trade/wire transfer.

Procedure (Tone: Operational, Assuring):

  1. **Verify & Confirm:** Authenticate. Use systems to confirm the trade/transfer execution date and time. "Yes, I can confirm the execution was successful on [Date] at [Time]."
  2. **Diagnose Delivery:** "Was the confirmation expected via postal mail or electronically through our portal?"
  3. **Resolve:** "I can immediately resend the confirmation electronically to your registered secure email, or confirm the documentation team has sent the postal copy." (Transfer to the back-office or trading settlement desk only if confirmation is missing from the system).
Scenario 14: Client demands the direct extension of a non-RM executive (e.g., Head of Compliance).

Procedure (Tone: Polite, Firm Boundary):

  1. **Verify Client & Listen:** Authenticate. Understand *why* they need to speak to the executive.
  2. **State Policy:** "Mr./Ms. [Client], due to the confidential nature of their roles, our executives do not take unannounced calls. I must act as your primary liaison."
  3. **Soft-Escalate:** "If you entrust me with the full details of your concern, I will prepare a detailed brief and ensure it reaches the executive's Chief of Staff for review and a coordinated response." (Never provide direct lines).
Scenario 15: Client needs service while traveling (e.g., cash withdrawal in London or Singapore).

Procedure (Tone: Global Concierge, Network Knowledge):

  1. **Verify & Identify Location:** Authenticate. "I see you are in [City]. Yes, we have a full service office there." (Refer to UBS, Julius Baer, Pictet, or EFG global locations).
  2. **Clarify Need:** "Do you require a pre-scheduled cash withdrawal, or is this a meeting request?"
  3. **Coordinate Service:** "I can contact the local branch team in [City] right now to ensure they are prepared for your arrival and coordinate any services you require." (Emphasize the seamless, global network).

Bonuses & Incentives: Performance Metrics

This section outlines the primary Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) that contribute to your semi-annual and annual bonus eligibility. Excellence in service, efficiency, and compliance are paramount.

Primary Performance Indicators (KPIs)

1. Client Satisfaction (CSAT)

Measured via post-call surveys specific to your interaction quality and follow-up speed.

  • **Target:** Maintain score above 95%.
  • **Impact:** Direct correlation to **Service Quality** component of bonus.

2. Efficient Resolution (FCR/AHT)

Focus on First Call Resolution (FCR) and minimizing Average Handle Time (AHT) for routine operational tasks.

  • **Target:** FCR > 80% (Operational Inquiries only).
  • **Impact:** Contributes to the **Productivity** component of bonus calculation.

3. Compliance & Risk

Zero tolerance for compliance failures (e.g., advising on investments, security breaches, failure to escalate Red Flags).

  • **Target:** Zero Compliance Incidents.
  • **Impact:** A mandatory gateway; any breach results in **immediate bonus disqualification**.

Secondary Incentives (RM Support)

These metrics are tied to supporting the Relationship Managers (RMs) and driving growth in the Wealth Management division:

Qualified Lead Generation

Successfully qualifying and routing new prospect inquiries (>$1M in assets) to the Business Development Manager (BDM).

CRM Documentation Quality

Completeness and timeliness of documenting all calls, especially those requiring complex follow-up or RM intervention.

Bonus Calculation Period

Performance is assessed quarterly for feedback, with the final bonus determination calculated **semi-annually** (June and December) based on the combined weighted average of all successful KPIs.

Terminology & Glossary (Quick Reference)

A comprehensive guide to the acronyms, specialized terms, and compliance shortcuts used across our banking curriculum.

Investment & Financial Terms

AUM
Assets Under Management: The total market value of assets the firm manages on behalf of clients. Key measure of a private bank's size.
Equities / Stocks
Represents ownership interest in a corporation. Generally considered high risk/high potential return.
Fixed Income / Bonds
A debt security issued by a corporation or government. The investor is lending money. Generally lower risk/lower return than equities.
Liquidity
The degree to which an asset can be quickly converted into cash without affecting its market price (e.g., cash is high liquidity; private equity is low liquidity).
Diversification
A risk management strategy that mixes a wide variety of investments within a portfolio to reduce volatility.
ETF
Exchange Traded Fund: A type of pooled investment security that trades like a common stock.
PE
Private Equity: Investments made directly into private companies. Typically illiquid and high risk/reward.
Lombard Loan
A specialized credit product where the client borrows money against their liquid investment assets (portfolio lending).

Compliance, Roles & Banking Acronyms

HNW / UHNW
High-Net-Worth / Ultra-High-Net-Worth: Clients with significant investable assets ($1M+ to $30M+).
RM / CRO
Relationship Manager / Client Relationship Officer: The primary client-facing contact responsible for holistic financial strategy and account oversight.
FATCA
Foreign Account Tax Compliance Act (US Law): Requires foreign financial institutions (like Swiss banks) to report US clients’ asset holdings to the US Treasury/IRS.
CRS
Common Reporting Standard: The global equivalent of FATCA, requiring automatic exchange of financial account information between participating tax jurisdictions.
FINMA
Financial Market Supervisory Authority (Switzerland): The independent regulator ensuring the stability and supervision of the Swiss financial market.
KYC / AML
Know Your Client / Anti-Money Laundering: Mandatory processes to verify client identity and source of wealth to prevent illegal activities.
ESG
Environmental, Social, and Governance: Criteria used in sustainable investing to measure a company's ethical and societal impact (Key USP for Mirabaud/Pictet).

Swiss Bank Scale Descriptors

Colossus (UBS)

Global Systemically Important Bank (G-SIB) with over $5T AUM post-merger. Offers full spectrum services (Wealth, Investment, Asset Mgmt).

Large Independent (Pictet, Julius Baer)

Independent firms with strong global reach ($400B-$600B+ AUM) specializing in Wealth and Asset Management. Highly focused, but not universal banks.

Mid-Sized Private / Boutique (EFG, Mirabaud, Cramer)

Smaller, often specialized or family-owned firms, highly competitive in specific niches (e.g., entrepreneurial clients, deep heritage). Provide extremely personalized service.
